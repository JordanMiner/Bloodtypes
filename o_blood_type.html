History

In 1927, Psychologist Takeji Furukawa was a teacher at a girls' school attached to a university. He began to question the school's entrance exam system, which was based solely on academic ability. He wanted a system that also considered the applicants' personalities. Based on this idea, he observed 11 people in his family and noted their blood types. There weren't any AB types in his family, so he lumped them together with A. And voila! From this incredibly tiny focus group, he wrote his famous thesis titled, "The Study of Temperament Through Blood Type." Even though Furukawa's theories were denied by academics in 1933, the theory was too popular to shoot down completely.

After WWII, discussions history the blood typing theory died down for some time. But in the 1970s, a journalist by the name of Masahiko Nomi, who was influenced by Takeji Furukawa, published a book history the relationships between blood types and personalities. This book became a bestseller and helped spread the theory more widely. By the 1980s, the topic was popping up in media like music, TV shows, and magazines. Furukawa's blood type theory had finally hit the big time!

Blood Type O Traits
People with this blood type are outgoing and make great leaders. They like to do things their own way, and they are resilient and flexible. They are outspoken and don't care for conventional thinking, which can sometimes initimidate others, and are often late to events.

  • These individuals are known for their ability to take charge when needed. They have a natural ability to know what needs to be done and do not hesitate to motivate others into joining alongside them. They are inspiring to others and are able to set the mood for groups of people.

    Natural Leaders

  • People with this blood type value honesty and have little respect for those who tell a lie or hide the truth. They prefer not to act a certain way just because it is an "industry standard" and will instead set new trends or practices wherever they go.

    Outspoken

  • People with this blood type are the first to take action, whether that's volunteering for extra work or answering a question. If they see a need for change, they will rise to the occasion to meet the challenge. They play to their strengths, which gives them the best chance at achieving success.

    Initiator

  • While the ever-optimist,these individuals have realistic views of themselves and their situations. They are able to logically weigh the losses and gains before acting, and see a purpose in each task performed. Instead of focusing on the small details, they like to see the bigger picture.

    Practical

  • Having too high of a degree of self-confidence can be a stumbling block for people with this blood type. Others may see them as arrogant and prideful when they assume that they are right in their chosen course of action. Because people with Blood Type O can assume that their way is best, they may be offended if others question their proposals.

    Arrogant

  • Because of their strong drive for success, these individuals can be ruthless in their attempts to achieve it. Sometimes they can be blinded by their desire to succeed that they dismiss the thoughts and feelings of others.

They are both powerful characters that are attracted to each other's strongest traits. That being said, this relationship needs balance in order to persist.Type O will need to learn to make compromises in the relationship and Type A needs to be understanding.

Although, Type O has their own need for recognition and attention, and Type B needs to make sure that they offer their support when their partner needs it most.

    This is an example of opposites attract. Type O is drawn to AB's adaptibility and spontaneity, while Type AB likes Type O's stability seriousness. That being said, these differences may cause conflicts, as the brutally honest Type O may become frusterated with Type AB's complexity, so mutual understanding is imperative for this relationship to work.
