History

In 1927, Psychologist Takeji Furukawa was a teacher at a girls' school attached to a university. He began to question the school's entrance exam system, which was based solely on academic ability. He wanted a system that also considered the applicants' personalities. Based on this idea, he observed 11 people in his family and noted their blood types. There weren't any AB types in his family, so he lumped them together with A. And voila! From this incredibly tiny focus group, he wrote his famous thesis titled, "The Study of Temperament Through Blood Type." Even though Furukawa's theories were denied by academics in 1933, the theory was too popular to shoot down completely.

After WWII, discussions history the blood typing theory died down for some time. But in the 1970s, a journalist by the name of Masahiko Nomi, who was influenced by Takeji Furukawa, published a book history the relationships between blood types and personalities. This book became a bestseller and helped spread the theory more widely. By the 1980s, the topic was popping up in media like music, TV shows, and magazines. Furukawa's blood type theory had finally hit the big time!

Blood Type A Traits
People with this blood type are described primarily as well-organized, but are not the best multi-taskers. They like to keep things neat but can be stubborn or get stressed out easily. They value harmony with others and work well in groups. They do not like fights and prefer to be in harmony with everyone, and they like to work in collaboration with others in the community. They tend to keep to themselves more so when they do not want to share their ideas or opinions.

  • These individuals are able to key in on the emotional needs of others. They often put others; interests before their own. They also want to make sure everyone is heard and is sensitive to the feelings of those around them.

    Compassionate

  • People with this blood type are great listeners and are very supportive of others. They want others to feel loved and accepted, and are happily invested in their relationships.

    Loyal

  • Many people of this blood type do not like to be in the spotlight, and are often introverted. They tend to keep their thoughts and feelings to themselves and would rather hear what others have to say.

    Reserved

  • These individuals are careful decision-makers and they hate taking risks. They like to plan everything and always assess risks and the possibility of something going wrong.

    Cautious

  • People with this blood type are highly focused and pay attention to very small details. They can be trusted to work very diligently, but they run into difficulty when they feel like they are being rushed or need to multi-task.

    Perfectionist

  • Because of their perfectionist tendencies, people with Type A blood are more likely to get stressed out easily. They also get easily stressed due to conflict or fights as they like to work in collaboration with others.

Compatibility
Like astrology, blood types can be used to determine if two people are compatible and foreshadow any conflicts that may occur in the relationship..

  • A × B

    Despite being opposites, both are able to complement each other. Both are spiritual and warm-hearted. That being said, this is not an easy match, since Type A desires order and stability and Type B prefers spontaneity. Additionally, Type B's social nature can be overwhelming to Type A who is much more introverted. Mutual understanding and clear communication is needed here.

    Blood Type B

  • A × O

    Those with Type O make for great partners for Type A. Type O offers a sense of security to Type A's anxiety and need for stability. They are both powerful characters that are attracted to each other's strongest traits. That being said, this relationship needs balance in order to persist.Type O will need to learn to make compromises in the relationship and Type A needs to be understanding.

    Blood Type O

  • A × AB

    Type A and Type AB have the chance to have a secure and loving friendship and relationship. The dependable and loving Type A will attract the realist being Type AB. That being said, issues can arise if Type AB leans too heavily towards Type B personality, making them too domineering and likely to start arguments.

    Blood Type AB

  • A × A

    Couples that are both Type A are both very intuitve of one another's feelings and have a mutual understanding of one another's wants. However, both of them can be very intense, which can cause irritation for both parties.
