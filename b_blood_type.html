History

In 1927, Psychologist Takeji Furukawa was a teacher at a girls' school attached to a university. He began to question the school's entrance exam system, which was based solely on academic ability. He wanted a system that also considered the applicants' personalities. Based on this idea, he observed 11 people in his family and noted their blood types. There weren't any AB types in his family, so he lumped them together with A. And voila! From this incredibly tiny focus group, he wrote his famous thesis titled, "The Study of Temperament Through Blood Type." Even though Furukawa's theories were denied by academics in 1933, the theory was too popular to shoot down completely.

After WWII, discussions history the blood typing theory died down for some time. But in the 1970s, a journalist by the name of Masahiko Nomi, who was influenced by Takeji Furukawa, published a book history the relationships between blood types and personalities. This book became a bestseller and helped spread the theory more widely. By the 1980s, the topic was popping up in media like music, TV shows, and magazines. Furukawa's blood type theory had finally hit the big time!

Blood Type B Traits
People with this blood type are known for their strong sense of curiosity and their creativity. Unfortunately, they are often stereotyped as being self-centered and a bit impulsive due to their free spirit.

  • These individuals are known for being independent thinkers and come up with solutions that nobody else can see. They throw their passion into everything they do, and this makes them great problem-solvers.

    Creative

  • People with this blood type are easy-going and little setbacks usually do not upset them. Because of their creativity, they simply find ways to move around their obstackles. They can easily craft back-up plans when things go wrong and are known for their flexibility.

    Relaxed

  • People with this blood type like to dance to the beat of their own drum and are the most likely to make big life changes on a whim to forge their own paths. They like to be free-spirited and dislike schedules or routines.

    Independent

  • When these individuals focus on something, they put all into it even if the goal is unachievable. They have a strong desire to be the best at anything they put their minds to. This can sometimes mean that they are bad at multi-tasking and may neglect other tasks to focus on their goal.

    driven

  • The free-spirited nature of these individuals also make them impulsive. Their dislike of planning makes them more likely to make decisions on the spot. Likewise, their poor attention to forethought, coupled with their brutal honesty, can lead to them saying what is on their mind without considering the reprecussions beforehand.

    Impulsive

  • If these individuals feel that they are being held back by someone or something, they will not hesitate to cast them aside.They are more likely than the other blood types to prioritize their own ambitions over other aspects of their life, and this can lead to them being self-centered.

Compatibility
Like astrology, blood types can be used to determine if two people are compatible and foreshadow any conflicts that may occur in the relationship.

  • B × A

    Despite being opposites, both are able to complement each other. Both are spiritual and warm-hearted. That being said, this is not an easy match, since Type A desires order and stability and Type B prefers spontaneity. Additionally, Type B's social nature can be overwhelming to Type A who is much more introverted. Mutual understanding and clear communication is needed here.

    Blood Type A

  • B × O

    These two idealists work well together and their relationship is both passionate and stable. Type B draws most of the attention on their talents and Type O helps lift their partner up and support their creative streak. Although, Type O has their own need for recognition and attention, and Type B needs to make sure that they offer their support when their partner needs it most.

    Blood Type O

  • B × AB

    Both Type B and Type AB are drawn to one another for their enthusiasm and passion. However, Type B needs to be understanding and adaptable of Type AB's unperdictability and Type AB needs to understand Type B's preference to go at their own pace. They'll need to have clear communication to ensure that they don't get in each other's way.

    Blood Type AB

  • B × B

    This relationship can either go very well or very poorly. Both are individualistic and social, and their ability to understand one another allows for a deep connection. That being said, both of them can be self-centered, which can lead to conflict if the issue is not resloved quickly.
